As the general election approaches, we can be absolutely certain that none of the main parties, in England at least, will address the central, existential question that is being asked in Northern Ireland, in Scotland, in Wales, and — rather more anxiously — in the Irish Republic. That is: how much longer can the United Kingdom go on being constituted as it is at present?

Some Scottish unionists are heaving a sigh of relief at the troubles now afflicting Nicola Sturgeon and the SNP but the root causes of the party’s success have not gone away — the sense that many Scots, who are not necessarily out and out separatists, are fed up with being governed from Westminster.
