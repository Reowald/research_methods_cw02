The head of the embattled Confederation of British Industry faced a backlash yesterday after calling on politicians to keep major tax cuts 'off the table'. Rain Newton-Smith, who has been struggling to rebuild the CBI's reputation after a sex scandal last year, said the Government should avoid 'pre-election giveaways'. But the comments are unlikely to find favour with Tory MPs clamouring for tax cuts to be announced in next month's budget as polls point to a mauling by Labour at the general election. ...read
