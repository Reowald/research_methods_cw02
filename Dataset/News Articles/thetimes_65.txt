Sir, I disagree with your leading article “Support on Campus” (Feb 16). Universities cannot be in effect the prime bearers of liability for the mental health of their students. They are already under pressure to run their own legal, police and justice services to manage sexual consent on campus. The distracting legal burden of pursuing diversity, equity and inclusion goals, with their chilling effects on free speech, is well documented. The result is an explosion of non-academic support and managerial staff, a by-product of the need to deflect litigation. In some universities this non-academic spending now exceeds the total spent on direct teaching (sadly, no one ever sues for bad teaching).
