Nothing in Richard Gaddes’s background suggested a stellar career in opera. Born and raised in the town of Wallsend, Tyne and Wear, he grew up at a time when there were still jobs to be had in the shipyards, where his father worked.

His life, however, took him in a very different direction. Both his parents sang in local choirs, and from an early age it was clear their son had inherited a musical talent. Like the Billy Elliot of ballet fame, he went south to study music, encouraged by his mother, though against his father’s wishes.
