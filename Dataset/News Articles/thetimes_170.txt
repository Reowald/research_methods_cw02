Plus: the forecast where you are

Britain invaded Corsica 230 years ago to establish a Mediterranean base in the fight against Revolutionary France.

The British vice-admiral Lord Hood planned to land at San Fiorenzo in Corsica, but in January 1794 his ships were battered by a gale as they neared the coast and he retreated. The invasion force returned the next month and landed troops at San Fiorenzo Bay ready to attack a formidable fort, Mortella Tower, above the bay. The next day two ships used a sea breeze to move in and bombard the tower’s thick walls but failed to make a breakthrough.
