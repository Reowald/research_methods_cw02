Sand eels are a key food source for many seabirds, including puffins

Sand eel fishing will be banned in Scottish waters before this year’s fishery season in a boost to the country’s seabirds.

Sand eels are an important food source for many species, including marine mammals, seabirds and predatory fish, and support the long-term sustainability and resilience of the marine ecosystem. RSPB Scotland said that ending industrial sand eel fishing was the “single greatest action” that could be taken to support the most vulnerable seabirds, including kittiwakes and puffins.

The Scottish government said the ban would come in before the 2024 fishery season, subject to parliamentary approval. It added that commercial fishing for sand eels was at present done entirely by European vessels.
