Homebuyers are facing yet more misery after US mortgage rates rose to their highest level in more than two months. The average rate on a 30-year fixed home loan reached 6.77 percent, up from 6.64 percent last week, according to figures from Government-backed lender Freddie Mac. It means somebody purchasing a $400,000 home with a five percent deposit faces paying an extra $700 per month than if they had bought two years ago.
