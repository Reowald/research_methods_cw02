‘OK, here we go, welcome to the Thunderdome,” the author Sloane Crosley says, self-mocking with glimpses of nervousness peeking through, as we begin to talk about her new book, Grief Is for People. This is far from Crosley’s first rodeo: at 45 she has written two novels already and three collections of witty autobiographical essays, including the bestselling I Was Told There’d Be Cake, which led to her being compared to Nora Ephron and David Sedaris.
