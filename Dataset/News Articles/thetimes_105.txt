This Test match was studded with three outstanding centuries by opening batsmen: Rohit Sharma on the first day and Ben Duckett on the second, while on the third and fourth days Yashasvi Jaiswal put together a stunning double century that involved him scoring 104 not out on Saturday before adding another 110 on Sunday. Duckett’s and Jaiswal’s innings rank among the finest of the modern Test era.

These performances typify the modern game in that openers no longer see their job as being to carefully construct a platform on which the rest of the side can build.
