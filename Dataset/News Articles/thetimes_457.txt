In the end Starmer had to go against a plan conceived by Ed Miliband

At the start of last month, Sir Keir Starmer was categorical. “The Tories are trying to weaponise the £28 billion,” he declared. “It’s a fight I want to have. I’m absolutely up for that.”

Only this week he told Times Radio that the sum for green investment under a Labour government was “desperately needed” to kick start a proper industrial strategy.

Yet, even as he protested that the pledge would not change, the Labour leader knew that the decision to dump it had been made. In fact, the U-turn announced on Thursday, under which the party is committed to spending only about a fifth of the promised total, had been in the making for many months, if not more than a year.
