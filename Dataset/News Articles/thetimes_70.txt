Jensen Huang told a conference in Dubai last week: “This is the beginning of a new industrial revolution. This is about the production, not of energy, not of food, but of intelligence. And every country needs to own the production of their own intelligence.”

Crucially, Nvidia, the Taiwanese-born billionaire’s company, has positioned itself at the heart of this “revolution”. It makes the powerful graphics processing units that are used to train and run artificial intelligence models and demand for its wares has been insatiable, pushing its stock market valuation within touching distance of $2 trillion.
