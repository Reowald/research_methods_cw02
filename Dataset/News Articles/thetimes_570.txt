Brussels has rowed back on its attempts to seize a larger chunk of the vast derivatives clearing market from the City of London in what has been hailed as “a victory for pragmatism”.

The dominant role of the UK’s financial centre in clearing euro-denominated derivatives — a market nominally worth about €500 trillion last year — has been a bone of contention since Britain voted to leave the European Union in 2016.
