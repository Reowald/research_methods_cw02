Polling gurus often warn us not to overestimate voters’ knowledge of the detail of politics, or underestimate their contempt for politicians. People can identify the party leaders and maybe a couple of frontbenchers, but beyond that minds go blank. As for policy, most are aware that the Tories are pro-business and cut taxes, that Labour is pro-NHS and backs unions (and the Union), and that the SNP wants to leave the UK for the EU.

Voters don’t follow the ins and outs of fiscal policy, let alone the shifts and turns of election promises, though. And why should they? Much of it is hogwash and spin. People have enough on their minds managing their ever-shrinking family budgets.
