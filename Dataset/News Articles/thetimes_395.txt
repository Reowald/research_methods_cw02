In 2009 a suspicious suicide in Tokyo led to the discovery of the Konkatsu Killer. The Killer was one Kanae Kijima, a woman in her mid-thirties, convicted of poisoning three men and suspected of killing four more. Her method didn’t vary. She tracked down lonely, middle-aged men on dating websites (konkatsu, hence the name), posed as the perfect housewife, swindled them out of millions of yen, then killed them. The “suicide” that landed her in prison was no suicide at all — she had cooked a beef stew, spiked it with sedatives, then poisoned her victim with carbon monoxide in a rented car.
