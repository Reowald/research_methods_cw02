The former UKIP leader said there is 'a lot we need to know' about the jabs and called for a 'massive investigation' into the damage they caused. The injections helped turn the tide on the pandemic by boosting immunity against severe illness from the virus, allowing for restrictions to be eased. However, like all vaccines and medications, serious side effects have been logged in very rare cases, including heart inflammation, blood clots and anaphylaxis. For this reason, the Government has a compensation scheme that dishes out six-figure sums to those who have been harmed by jabs, including those offered during the Covid crisis. ...read
