Companies in all sectors, including manufacturing, are under pressure to set and follow environmental, social and governance goals

Environmental, social and governance must evolve to avoid cynicism and to become relevant to smaller companies, research concludes

Companies, regulators and stock markets have been advised to change their approach to environmental, social and governance issues to prevent the subject from becoming a “tick-box” exercise.

ESG is at risk of losing its purpose and potential to be useful because of an over-reliance on data rather than action, according to new research. It could fail to drive the impact it was intended to have on climate change and on society, it is claimed, and both existing and pending regulations are failing to take into account small and medium-sized businesses because they have been designed by and for large companies.
