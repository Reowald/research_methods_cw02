David Baddiel says whatever is on his mind — like a child does. This, he insists, is not a bad thing. “Our world has a paralysing self-consciousness where nobody says anything that is true to who they are any more,” he says. “And where can you find that honesty? In a child.” Baddiel’s wife has suggested that he could, perhaps, say the second thing that comes to his mind. “But I’ve monetised it,” he says of his brashness. “I’m a limited performer, but I’m very good at being myself.”
