The parents of a student who killed herself said that “universities have become more like a survival course than a place of learning” as young people struggle to cope with rising pressure and anxiety.

Natasha Abrahart, a second-year physics student at the University of Bristol, was found dead in her student flat in April 2018. The high-achieving 20-year-old, who dreamt of being a research scientist, was suffering from social anxiety so acute that she felt unable to speak to strangers and give presentations and oral assessments that were part of her course.
