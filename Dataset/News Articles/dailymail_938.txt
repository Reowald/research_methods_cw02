Dozens of houses along the coastline in Hemsby, Norfolk, have been torn down in recent months after high tides and strong winds caused the collapse of a stretch of private access road. Photos in October showed homes perilously close to toppling over the edge sparking Great Yarmouth Council to call in demolition workers. Five were torn down as the local authority explained the decision, taken with the owners' permission, was because the homes were 'not structurally sound and are unsafe'.
