Ray Davies used to get so nervous before shows that he would hide at the side of the stage, waiting until the curtains rose, to run in from the wings. Once, his band, the Kinks, were opening for the Beatles and Davies, then 20, felt a pull on his back when it was his cue to rush out. “It was Paul McCartney,” he recalls, which is a funny story, but I am not sure how funny Davies finds it, even decades on.

“Because I never wanted to be the lead singer,” he explains, quite dolefully. “My brother is much prettier than I am. He should have been the lead singer.”
