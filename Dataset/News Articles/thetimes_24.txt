James Smith and Sons has long been a feature of New Oxford Street in London

“The rain, it raineth on the just and also on the unjust fella: but chiefly on the just, because the unjust steals the just’s umbrella.”

If the words of the Victorian judge Lord Bowen of Colwood are true, then you will find the just fella shopping at James Smith and Sons on New Oxford Street.

If you ask a London cabbie for “the umbrella shop” they will know to bring you here. Admittedly, it is hard to miss as the word “umbrellas” stands in large, red letters on the shop building’s façade, but James Smith’s is well known to Londoners as it nears the end of its second century of trading.

James Smith founded his shop in 1830, originally at Foubert’s Place off of Regent
