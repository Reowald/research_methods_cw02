Stuck in a wine rut? Me too. As winter drags on, the same old, same old malbec-shiraz-merlot merry-go-round starts to grate. It’s time we all rebooted by diving into warming winter reds made from more unusual grapes and often from more unexpected places that few of us have heard of. Apart from colour and price, it’s grape variety that dictates our wine choices, ahead of country or region, hence my line-up here.

A good place to start your off-piste red wine journey is with cool-climate Austria and its vibrant, crunchy, violet and forest berry-fruited zweigelt grape.
