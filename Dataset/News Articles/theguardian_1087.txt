In a Bomb Factory, 1941 (Picture Post 882 – Making a Beautiful Bomb) Born into a working-class family in Blackfriars, Hardy was entirely self-taught and worked his way up from lab assistant to become an influential press and documentary photographer. His long-time collaborator, Tom Hopkinson, editor of Picture Post (1940-50) described Hardy as ‘the nearest to an all-round cameraman I ever worked with. There were few assignments – except perhaps theatre and ballet – on which I wouldn’t have wished to send him’. Here we see a munitions worker rolling a bomb with her foot
