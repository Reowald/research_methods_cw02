As he was one of the most talented all-round cricketers ever to play the game and yet took part in only seven Test matches, Mike Procter could well have been forgiven for becoming embittered. Instead, when quizzed in retirement about his regrets, he surprised his audiences by stating, “I didn’t do enough. I allowed apartheid to go on around me.”

Procter, a flaxen-haired fast bowler and punchy and stylish batsman, was coming into his prime years when South Africa’s tour to Australia in 1971-72 was called off owing to its government’s policy of segregation of races.
