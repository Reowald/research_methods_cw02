Campaigners began 'spamming' the museum's social media accounts after a Santiago-based influencer encouraged his one million followers to flood them with 'return the moai' comments. The museum has two moai statues which were taken from Rapa Nui (Easter Island) by British surveyors in 1868. There has since been longstanding demands for the statues to be returned to the Chilean territory.
