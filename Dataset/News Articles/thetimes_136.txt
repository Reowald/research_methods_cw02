I have heard some shocking stories about the miners’ strike, but the one about Dave Roper’s dead baby in Miners’ Strike: A Frontline Story almost beggars belief. Roper was a striking miner in South Yorkshire, whose wife went into labour in the summer of 1984. Their son, Adam, died a week later. But when Roper applied for a funeral grant he was informed he didn’t qualify — because he was on strike.
