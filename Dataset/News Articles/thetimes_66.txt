The landmark court ruling that Bristol University had discriminated against Natasha Abrahart, the student found dead in her flat in 2018, not only brings relief to her parents, who sued the university for not making sufficient arrangements to deal with her anxiety; it also imposes on all universities in England and Wales the obligation to ensure that all students suffering from mental illness, considered a disability, are properly helped to cope with their condition. This is both right and humane, though it will demand a considerable shift in resources and attitudes.
