Oil Bunkering #9, Niger Delta, Nigeria, 2016 Despite gaining independence in October of 1960, much of Nigeria’s oil wealth continues to be diverted outside the country. As a result, poor communities have begun pirating crude oil from pipelines through illegal ‘bunkering’. Tapping into pipelines, makeshift micro-refineries are set up on a temporary or semi-permanent basis to turn the crude into low-grade fuel. Highly dangerous, these systems – and the broken pipelines that feed them – leak volumes of crude oil and toxic by-products into the surrounding forests and waterways
