Scott Vincent founded Digital Futures to help youngsters who come from disadvantaged backgrounds like his to launch a new career

Scott Vincent can pinpoint the moment that changed the direction of his life. He was 20 and had recently moved to London from Canberra, where he had spent his teenage years supporting his mother and younger brother after his father was killed by the Australian mafia.

He wanted to put the trauma behind him and “reinvent” himself. Top of the to-do list was to land a job in financial services, but without a university degree it was proving difficult. He was relying on part-time bar work and DJ gigs to make ends meet when he was asked to attend an interview for an analyst’s job at Barclays. It was here that he met Colin Tayler, the hiring manager.
