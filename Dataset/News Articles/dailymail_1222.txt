Stealing a cat or dog is set to become a specific offence, with up to five years behind bars, under a new law being backed by Downing Street. This comes as thousands of pet owners backed calls for a new law to make councils check dead cats and dogs found on roads for microchips - as it is currently being a 'postcode lottery' of whether owners can 'say goodbye properly'. Under current laws, when an animal is stolen, in law, it is viewed as the theft of 'property'.
