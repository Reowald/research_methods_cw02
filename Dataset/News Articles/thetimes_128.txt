The Spanish took charge of this fascinating London Philharmonic concert, supplying conductor, soloist and world premiere: a “not quite” piano concerto called Ciudad sin Sueño (City That Does Not Sleep) by the 39-year-old Francisco Coll. I have enjoyed Coll’s extrovert, energetic pieces in the past but sometimes felt their structure a bit random. This 20-minute piece, although described as a “fantasia”, is much more organised.

A bright, brittle opening and stomping, syncopated finale enclose a slow, emotionally charged central section.
