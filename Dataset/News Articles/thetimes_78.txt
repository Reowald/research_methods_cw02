Crowdfunding sector calls on regulator to review the impact of rules designed to protect vulnerable retail investors

The crowdfunding industry is calling on the City regulator to review the impact of investor protections introduced a year ago amid concerns that the flow of funds to promising companies is being undermined.

The UK Crowdfunding Association has asked the Financial Conduct Authority to investigate whether rules designed to protect vulnerable retail investors may go too far and could threaten the viability of business models in the sector.

The association represents 25 British investment crowdfunding and peer-to-peer lending platforms that collectively connect tens of thousands of individual investors with companies seeking finance.
