Stephen Fry and Lena Dunham are the double-act you never knew you needed in this low-key tearjerker about repressed grief and family tensions. Adapted from Lily Brett’s novel Too Many Men, Treasure stars Dunham as the newly divorced New York journalist Ruth Rothwax who, in 1991, decides to drag her reluctant father Edek (Fry) on a ramshackle road trip around post-Iron Curtain Poland. There, in the name of catharsis, they will visit Edek’s old family home, his local village and the death camp of Auschwitz where, as a young Jew, he was separated forever from his loved ones.
