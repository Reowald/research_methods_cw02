I don’t know how familiar you are with the national food days in the calendar. We’re not as bad as America (hello National Buffet Day), but we’ve got our share of ludicrous celebrations, from last weekend’s Yorkshire Pudding Day to Prosecco Day (on August 13, since you ask).

Most of these are wishful thinking by ambitious PR teams, but there’s one culinary day that I always get behind, and not just because it’s got the oldest, most authoritative backing there is. Shrove Tuesday (or Pancake Day, as it’s known in these more secular times) marks the last day before Lent, when households would use up their eggs and fats before 40 days of fasting.
