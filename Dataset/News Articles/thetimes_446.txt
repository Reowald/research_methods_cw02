‘The tributaries that go into the Severn, if you went today they’re devoid of any fish. They are just terrible, there’s nothing in them,” said Andy Jones, an angler who has been fishing on Great Britain’s longest river and its tributaries since he was six years old.

Jones is one of many living along the Severn who fear it faces a fate similar to that of the River Wye in Herefordshire. Last year the Wye’s ecological status was downgraded because of pressure from a “manure mountain” created by chicken farms.
