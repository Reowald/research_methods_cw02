In many ways, the hamfisted introduction and sudden death of the Super Series are symptomatic of Mark Dodson’s reign as chief executive of the Scottish Rugby Union.

The outgoing (in every sense) Lancastrian will argue until his dying day that bringing about the new “part-time professional” tier in 2018 had to happen through imposition rather than consent, on account of the clubs having been unable to reach consensus on the way ahead.

The reality is, however, that the Super6 (as it was then) was very much a Dodson baby, from conception, through gestation and a difficult birth to its eventual abandonment as part of the new “male performance pathway designed to retain the country’s position in the world’s top five nations over the next decade”.
