If the shortage of houses is critical for everyone, it’s at screaming pitch for disabled people. I did some unscientific research this weekend, trawling hundreds of properties for sale either on the ground floor or with a lift.

I found precisely nothing. Bungalows had several steps. There were no wet rooms; corridors were cramped; doorways were narrow. Were I desperate to move — and I’m supremely fortunate: my husband and I are plunging our life savings into building a tiny accessible house in the garden — I would be desperate.
