It is slightly surreal to read the awestruck coverage from when the euro was launched 25 years ago, on January 1, 1999. There were quasi-religious celebrations among the 11 founding states ( Greece fatefully joined two years later). When euro coins and banknotes finally launched three years later, fireworks streaked through dark winter skies. Buildings were illuminated in gold stars. Politicians made speeches heralding the dawn of a new era of peace and plenty. Even British journalists, especially at the BBC , got in on the act.
