The epiphany happened in Bognor Regis, January 2023. That day Yard Act had already played one gig. The previous year they had played more than 200. The Leeds-based band were working hard to make money in a cash-strapped industry, desperately trying to maintain their new and precious popularity, driven largely by a BBC6 Music audience who latched on to their post-punk, post-Brexit poetry and wry, dry lyrics about gentrification and Poundland.

James Smith, 33, the frontman and lyricist, stalked out on stage night after night, speak-singing over the music in his flat Manc drawl.
